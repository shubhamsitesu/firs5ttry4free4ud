What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value calculated from your weight and height that provides a reliable indicator of body fatness for most people. It is used to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems.

Standard BMI Formula

BMI = weight (kg) / (height in meters)2

For adults (18+ years):

  • Underweight: BMI < 18.5
  • Normal: 18.5 - 24.9
  • Overweight: 25 - 29.9

Waist Circumference Matters

For Indians, waist measurement is equally important:

  • Men: Keep below 90cm (35.5 inches)
  • Women: Keep below 80cm (31.5 inches)

Higher waist circumference increases risk of diabetes and heart disease.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI is a useful screening tool but doesn't directly measure body fat. It may overestimate body fat in athletes and underestimate it in older persons or those who have lost muscle.

Is BMI accurate for Indians?

While BMI is a useful screening tool, Indians tend to have higher body fat percentage at lower BMI levels compared to Western populations. Many experts suggest using lower BMI cut-offs for Indians to identify health risks.

What is the ideal BMI for Indian men and women?

For Indians, the ideal BMI range is generally considered to be between 18.5 to 22.9 kg/mÂ² for both men and women. This is slightly lower than the international standard of 18.5-24.9 due to our body composition differences.

How often should I check my BMI?

For most adults, checking BMI every 3-6 months is sufficient unless you're actively trying to lose or gain weight.

Does BMI differ for men and women?

The BMI calculation is the same for both genders, but women tend to have more body fat than men at the same BMI.
